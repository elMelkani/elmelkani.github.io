I am a postdoctoral researcher in Prof. Denis Bartolo’s group at ENS Lyon. My work lies at the interface of theoretical soft matter, active matter, and biophysics, with a focus on collective dynamics in biological and synthetic non-equilibrium systems.

I combine analytical tools such as hydrodynamics, kinetic theory, and non-Hermitian/topological methods with computational approaches including data analysis, machine learning, and agent-based simulations. Broadly, I am interested in using these methods both to model biological systems and to design metamaterials with programmable dynamical properties. I am currently working on a project studying the shape dynamics of self-organized fish schools.

I received my PhD in Physics from the University of Oregon in 2024, where my dissertation studied activity and non-reciprocity in soft matter using ideas from non-Hermitian physics. Previously, I completed an undergraduate degree in Engineering Physics at IIT Bombay in 2019, working on quantum information and quantum optics.
